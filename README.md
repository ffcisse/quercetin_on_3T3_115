# Effects of Quercetin on Lipid Accumulation in 3T3-L1 Cells

## Introduction
This project investigates the effects of quercetin, a bioactive flavonoid, on adipogenesis and lipid accumulation in 3T3-L1 cells. Obesity is closely linked to adipocyte differentiation and lipid storage, making it important to explore compounds that may regulate this process.

The goal of this study was to determine how the **timing of quercetin exposure** during different stages of adipogenesis (induction, maturation, and maintenance) influences lipid accumulation and cell morphology.

## Skills
- **Cell Culture Techniques** (3T3-L1 adipocyte model)
- **Oil Red O (ORO) Staining** (lipid quantification)
- **ImageJ** (image-based quantification)
- **Bright-field Microscopy**
- **Experimental Design & Data Analysis**


## Features
- Controlled experimental design with multiple treatment timepoints  
- Quantitative analysis of lipid accumulation using ImageJ  
- Comparison across control and experimental conditions  
- Morphological analysis of adipocyte differentiation  
- Investigation of timing-dependent drug effects on adipogenesis  


## Process
1. Cultured 3T3-L1 cells in 6-well plates  
2. Established control and experimental groups  
3. Administered quercetin at three stages:
   - Induction  
   - Maturation  
   - Maintenance  
4. Performed adipogenic differentiation protocol  
5. Stained lipid droplets using Oil Red O  
6. Captured microscopy images of each well  
7. Quantified lipid accumulation using ImageJ  
8. Compared lipid accumulation across conditions  


## Results
- Early exposure to quercetin (induction stage) **reduced lipid accumulation significantly**  
- Mid-stage exposure showed **moderate lipid accumulation**  
- Late-stage exposure resulted in **higher lipid accumulation than baseline**  
- ImageJ analysis showed clear differences in:
  - Area stained (lipid accumulation)
  - Integrative density across wells :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}  
- Morphological analysis revealed:
  - Smaller, clustered cells with early treatment  
  - Larger lipid droplets with later treatment  
  - Signs of cellular stress and lysis in late-stage exposure  

👉 Overall finding:  
**Quercetin’s effect on adipogenesis is strongly dependent on timing of exposure**
